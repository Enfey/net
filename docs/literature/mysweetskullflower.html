My Sweet Skullflower

My abject think-flesh, my sweet skullflower, what a wickedly noetic implement you are! This little thought factory inhabits a blessed vessel, a suit of meaty armour to reify its logical considerations.

How beautiful such a thing is, to produce such fine logic closed under extrinsic abstractions, when it is conceived not knowing of such rules. A transformative device, a relentless pursuant of knowledge of the immediate world. A biological whetstone, cyclically eliminating weakness and imperfection. Is this not the pinnacle of creation?

Bask in the brilliance of complication! Though beautiful, the wrinkled material by which our will is writ unto the universe is not flawless. Our skullflowers make dictators of the weak, seed vile corruption in purity, and perpetuate ungodly, self-serving dogma. The totality of misery endured by humans is on its immaterial hands!

I have suffered by it. Agonising patterns of a recursive disposition, externalised as pangs of obligation, push me deeper into an ocean of volumetric dejection. Thus the head is a divergent clump of matter. It is neither wholly imperfect nor perfect; it is bitter and sweet, dirty and pure. I neither drown in the ocean nor rise from it. Fathom the permutations of this discord ! Inescapable, endless limbo is our sublime prize.

For such divinity to be at odds with itself is maddening. We shall try to understand this.

Did you get all that? Good. [2]



I, the vessel of this capricious mind-meat, walk this earth with gratitude in my heart. So I thank you my sweet, fragile skullflower. We will wither and wilt, and be thawed from mortality. Self-extrication from our earthly manacles is truly inevitable. Teetering on the edge of the irrevocable oblivion, a broken choir of grief and appetence often subjugates the self. A final litany. We will know of no such sentiment. Nestled in the brutal embrace of finality I trust to remember my doctrine:

'That I am, that I was, that I will be. This is enough. This is my axiom.'

[1] : Just a note on thought generation function. It is not injective given that different combinations of sensory inputs and internal state can lead to the same thought. I'm sure you can think of an example for this :)
